What Is ROI and How To Use It

Learn what Return on Investment (ROI) means, how to calculate it, and how to use it to compare investment opportunities.

What is ROI?

ROI (Return on Investment) measures the efficiency of an investment by comparing the gain or loss relative to the cost. It's expressed as a percentage and helps you compare different investment opportunities on equal footing.

ROI Formula

Formula
ROI = ((Final Value - Initial Investment) / Initial Investment) × 100

This simple formula works for any investment where you know the starting value and ending value.

Example Calculation

1

Initial Investment

You invest $1,000 in a stock.

2

Final Value

After one year, the stock is worth $1,200.

3

Calculate ROI

ROI = (($1,200 - $1,000) / $1,000) × 100 = 20%

What This Means

A 20% ROI means you earned $20 for every $100 invested. Positive ROI = profit; negative ROI = loss.

Annualized ROI

When comparing investments of different durations, use annualized ROI to put them on equal footing.

Annualized Formula
Annualized ROI = ((1 + ROI)^(1/years) - 1) × 100

For example, a 50% return over 3 years = approximately 14.5% annualized return.

Beyond the Numbers

ROI is a useful starting point, but smart investors also consider:

Risk

Higher potential returns usually come with higher risk. A 20% ROI from bonds differs vastly from 20% from crypto.

Time Horizon

A 20% return in 1 year is very different from 20% over 10 years. Always annualize for comparison.

Costs & Fees

Management fees, transaction costs, and taxes reduce your actual return. Calculate net ROI after costs.

Key Takeaway

ROI is a powerful tool for comparing investments, but always consider risk, time horizon, and fees alongside the headline number.
