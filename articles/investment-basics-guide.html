Build a Simple Investment Framework

Great portfolios are boring. Follow these three pillars and let automation do the heavy lifting.

1

Map your goals by horizon

Write down targets for short (≤2 years), medium (3–7 years), and long-term (10+ years) goals. Assign a base currency and inflation assumption to each.

2

Ring-fence your safety buffer

Keep 3–6 months of expenses in insured cash products before increasing market exposure. Separate emergency savings from brokerage accounts.

3

Automate and rebalance

Schedule contributions for payday and rebalance quarterly back to target weights. Increase contributions whenever your income rises.

Due Diligence

Always confirm regulator registration (SEC, FCA, KNF, etc.) and investor compensation schemes (SIPC, FSCS, BFG). Screenshot fee tables before signing to avoid hidden spreads.

Model Portfolios You Can Copy

Use these allocations as a starting point. Rebalance annually and adjust currency exposure to your base currency.

Conservative (0–3 years)

Preserve capital while earning real yield on cash you need soon.

  • 50% insured deposits / money-market
  • 30% short-duration government bonds
  • 20% ultra-short bond ETFs / T-bills

Balanced (3–7 years)

Blend growth and stability for medium-term goals.

  • 15% cash buffer
  • 45% global equity ETFs
  • 25% investment-grade bonds
  • 15% international/small-cap

Growth (10+ years)

Maximize equity exposure for long-term wealth.

  • 70% global equities (broad market)
  • 20% thematic/small-cap tilt
  • 10% bonds for dry powder
Key Takeaway

Consistency beats timing. Set up automatic contributions, stay diversified, and let compound returns do the heavy lifting over time.
